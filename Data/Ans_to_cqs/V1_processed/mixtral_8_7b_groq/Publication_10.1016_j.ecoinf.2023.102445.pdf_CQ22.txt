The performance of the deep learning model for object detection, particularly in the context of insect detection, is evaluated using several metrics. These include Average Precision (AP), mean Average Precision (mAP), Intersection over Union (IoU), precision, recall, and time.

Average Precision (AP) is calculated as the area under the Precision-Recall curve. Precision is the ratio of true positive detections to the total number of positive detections, while recall is the ratio of true positive detections to the total number of actual positives. The mAP is the average AP calculated across all classes at different IoU thresholds, spanning from 0.5 to 0.95 in increments of 0.05. IoU is a measure of the overlap between the predicted bounding box and the ground truth bounding box.

In addition to these metrics, the time taken for the entire process, including preprocessing, network inference, and post-processing, is also considered. This helps in evaluating the real-time performance of the model, which is crucial for applications like pest detection in agriculture.
