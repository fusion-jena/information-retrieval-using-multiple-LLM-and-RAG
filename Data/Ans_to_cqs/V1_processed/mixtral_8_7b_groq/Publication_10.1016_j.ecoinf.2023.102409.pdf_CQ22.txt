The performance of a deep learning model, such as the one discussed in the context provided, is evaluated using several metrics. These include accuracy, precision, recall, and Intersection over Union (IoU).

Accuracy is a ratio of correctly predicted data points to the total number of data points in the dataset. It gives an overall idea of how well the model is performing.

Precision, on the other hand, measures the model's ability to discriminate between negative samples. It ranges from 0 to 1, with values closer to 1 indicating better discrimination.

Recall, also known as sensitivity, measures the model's ability to identify positive samples. Like precision, it ranges from 0 to 1, with values closer to 1 indicating better identification.

Intersection over Union (IoU) is a metric used specifically for semantic segmentation tasks. It determines the proportion of intersection to union between the true labels and the predicted values. MIoU, or Mean IoU, computes IoU for each category separately and then averages the results across all categories. This provides a more nuanced understanding of the model's performance in segmenting different categories.

In addition to these, Mean Pixel Accuracy (MPA) is another metric used in semantic segmentation. It measures the accuracy of pixel-level categorization by calculating the ratio of correctly categorized pixels to the total number of pixels.
