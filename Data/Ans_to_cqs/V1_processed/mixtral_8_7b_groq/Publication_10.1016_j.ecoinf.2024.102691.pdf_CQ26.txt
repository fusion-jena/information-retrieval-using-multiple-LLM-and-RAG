In general, data bias can be addressed during preprocessing using various techniques such as stratified sampling, oversampling, undersampling, and diverse data collection.

Stratified sampling is a method of sampling where the population is divided into homogeneous subgroups or 'strata', and a sample is taken from each stratum. This ensures that the sample is representative of the entire population, reducing bias.

Oversampling is a technique used when the minority class is underrepresented in the dataset. It involves creating more instances of the minority class, either by duplication or synthetic generation, to balance the dataset.

Undersampling is the opposite of oversampling and is used when the majority class is overrepresented. It involves reducing the number of instances of the majority class to balance the dataset.

Diverse data collection involves gathering data from various sources, domains, or perspectives to ensure that the dataset is representative of the real-world scenarios and reduce bias.
