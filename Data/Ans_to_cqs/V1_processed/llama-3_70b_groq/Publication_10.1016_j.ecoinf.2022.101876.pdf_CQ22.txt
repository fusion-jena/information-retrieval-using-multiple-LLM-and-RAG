The metrics used to evaluate the performance of the deep learning model (MegaDetector) are:

1. TPR (True Positive Rate or Recall): TP/(TP + FN)
2. FDR (False Discovery Rate): FP/(FP + TP)
3. FOR (False Omission Rate): FN/(FN + TN)
4. ACC (Accuracy): (TP + TN)/(TP + TN + FP + FN)
5. ERR (Error Rate): (FP + FN)/(TP + TN + FP + FN)
6. F1 Score (Harmonic mean between TPR and PPV): (2*TP)/((2*TP) + FP + FN)
7. MCC (Matthews Correlation Coefficient): ((TP*TN)-(FP*FN))/(SQRT((TP + FP)* (TP + FN)*(TN + FP)*(TN + FN)))

These metrics are used to evaluate the performance of the MegaDetector model in detecting wildlife in camera trap images from Arctic Alaska.
