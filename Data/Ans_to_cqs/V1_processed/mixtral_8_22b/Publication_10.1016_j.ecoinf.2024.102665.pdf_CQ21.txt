After the model training process, several postprocessing steps can be performed depending on the specific requirements of the project. Some common postprocessing steps include:

1. Saliency maps: These visualizations help identify which parts of the input data had the most significant impact on the model's predictions. They can provide insights into how the model makes decisions and highlight any potential biases.

2. Metrics calculation: Calculating various performance metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score helps evaluate the effectiveness of the trained model. These metrics can be calculated by comparing the predicted outputs against ground truth labels.

3. Confusion matrix: A confusion matrix is a table layout that allows visualizing the performance of a classification algorithm. It shows the number of correct and incorrect predictions made by the model, broken down by class. This can help identify patterns in misclassifications and guide further improvements to the model.
