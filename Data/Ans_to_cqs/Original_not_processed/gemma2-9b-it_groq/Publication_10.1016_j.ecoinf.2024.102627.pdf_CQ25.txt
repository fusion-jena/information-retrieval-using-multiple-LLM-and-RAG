The deep learning model described in the text is used for **set-valued prediction**, also known as **set-valued classification**. 

Its purpose is to predict likely species assemblages worldwide based on presence-only observations.  

The model works by:

1. Training a deep species distribution model (SDM) on presence-only data for all species combined.
2. Using the trained SDM to predict a set of species labels for each location by thresholding the categorical probabilities associated with each species.


Essentially, the model aims to learn the relationships between environmental factors and the presence of different species, allowing it to predict which species are likely to occur together in a given location.
