While the provided text focuses on specific applications of deep learning in remote sensing, it doesn't delve into the general preprocessing steps for training these models. 

However, common preprocessing steps for deep learning models generally include:

* **Data Cleaning:** This involves handling missing values, removing duplicates, and correcting inconsistencies in the dataset.

* **Normalization:** Scaling features to a common range (e.g., 0 to 1 or -1 to 1) can improve model performance.

* **Feature Engineering:**  Creating new features from existing ones can sometimes enhance model accuracy. This might involve extracting relevant information from images, text, or other data types.

* **Data Augmentation:**  Artificially increasing the size and diversity of the training dataset by applying transformations like rotations, flips, and crops can help prevent overfitting.

* **Encoding Categorical Data:**  Converting categorical variables (e.g., class labels) into numerical representations (e.g., one-hot encoding) is often necessary for deep learning models.


Remember that the specific preprocessing steps will vary depending on the nature of the data and the chosen deep learning model.
