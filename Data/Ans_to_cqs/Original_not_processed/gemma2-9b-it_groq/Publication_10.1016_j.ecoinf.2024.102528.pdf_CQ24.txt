Common strategies for addressing randomness in deep learning include:

* **Setting a fixed random seed:** This ensures that the same random numbers are generated each time the model is run, leading to reproducible results.
* **Using techniques like dropout:** Dropout randomly "drops out" (ignores) a percentage of neurons during training, preventing overfitting and introducing some randomness into the learning process.
* **Data augmentation:** This involves applying random transformations (e.g., rotations, flips, crops) to the training data, increasing the diversity of the training set and mitigating the impact of randomness in the data itself.
