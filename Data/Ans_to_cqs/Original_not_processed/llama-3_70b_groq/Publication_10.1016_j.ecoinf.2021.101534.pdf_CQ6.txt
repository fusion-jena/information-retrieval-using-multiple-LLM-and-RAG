According to the provided context, the preprocessing steps involved before training a deep learning model are:

1. Audio standardization: The recordings are standardized to account for variations in recording equipment, settings, and weather conditions. This involves setting a sampling rate of 32 kHz with a 100 Hz high-pass filter.

2. Segmentation: The labeled sounds in a recording are clipped into fixed-sized input images for model training.

3. Data augmentation: This involves transforming the data to increase the variability of the audio data in the training dataset. The default data augmentation used in this context includes scaling, color space adjustments, and Mosaic augmentation.

4. Spectrogram conversion: The audio data is converted into spectrograms, which are then used as input images for model training.

These preprocessing steps are applied to the audio data before training the YOLOv5s model.
