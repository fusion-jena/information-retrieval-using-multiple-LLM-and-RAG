The purpose of the deep learning models in the given context is object detection, specifically for the detection of Pacific oysters. Two deep learning models were selected for comparison, YOLOv5 and Faster R-CNN (FR-CNN). YOLOv5 is a lightweight approach that identifies objects directly through a single pass, while FR-CNN is a multi-stage network approach based on identifying potential regions, categorizing them, and discarding some inappropriate regions.

Three variants of the YOLOv5 model were also tested: YOLOv5 small, YOLOv5 medium, and TPH-YOLOv5. YOLOv5s, as the smallest model, performs best in terms of precision. TPH-YOLOv5 is an extension of the YOLOv5s model with further pre-training on RPAS (Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems) specific imagery. However, the results indicated that detection of Pacific oysters does not demand the complexity of the larger YOLOv5m model and using the TPH-YOLOv5 model pre-trained with RPAS data is not beneficial in this instance.

Therefore, the deep learning models are used for the specific purpose of detecting Pacific oysters in a given image or dataset, with a focus on finding the most efficient and precise model.
