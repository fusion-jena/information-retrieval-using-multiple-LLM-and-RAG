The performance of the deep learning model can be evaluated using various metrics, including accuracy, precision, recall, and others.

Accuracy is a common metric that measures the proportion of correct predictions out of the total number of predictions made by the model. It is calculated as the number of true positives and true negatives divided by the total number of predictions.

Precision, on the other hand, measures the proportion of true positives among all positive predictions made by the model. It is calculated as the number of true positives divided by the sum of true positives and false positives.

Recall, also known as sensitivity, measures the proportion of true positives that were correctly identified by the model among all actual positive instances. It is calculated as the number of true positives divided by the sum of true positives and false negatives.

These metrics are useful in evaluating the performance of the deep learning model, especially in binary classification problems. However, in multi-class classification problems, additional metrics such as F1 score, Cohen's kappa, and area under the curve (AUC) may be used.

The F1 score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall, which takes into account both metrics' importance. It ranges from 0 to 1, with a higher score indicating better performance.

Cohen's kappa measures the agreement between the predicted and actual classes, taking into account the possibility of the agreement occurring by chance. It ranges from -1 to 1, with a higher score indicating better performance.

The AUC measures the model's ability to distinguish between positive and negative classes. It ranges from 0 to 1, with a higher score indicating better performance.

These metrics are used to evaluate the performance of the deep learning model, and the choice of metric depends on the specific problem and the business objective. It is essential to choose the appropriate metric to evaluate the model's performance accurately.
