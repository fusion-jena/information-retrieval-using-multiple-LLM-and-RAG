The Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) is a measure of the differences between the predicted and observed values. It is calculated as the square root of the average of the squared differences between the predicted and observed values. A lower RMSE indicates a better fit of the model to the data.

The Standard Deviation is a measure of the spread of a dataset. It is calculated as the square root of the variance of the dataset.

The Squared Chi-square Distance is a measure of the difference between two probability distributions. It is calculated as the sum of the squared differences between the observed and expected frequencies, divided by the expected frequencies.
