In the context of machine learning, some techniques used to address data bias include stratified sampling, oversampling, undersampling, and diverse data collection.

Stratified sampling is a method of sampling where the population is divided into homogeneous subgroups or strata, and a sample is taken from each stratum. This method ensures that each subgroup is proportionally represented in the sample, reducing bias.

Oversampling and undersampling are techniques used to balance classes in a dataset. Oversampling involves increasing the number of instances in the minority class, while undersampling involves decreasing the number of instances in the majority class. These techniques can help reduce bias caused by an imbalanced dataset.

Diverse data collection involves gathering data from a variety of sources and ensuring that the data is representative of the population. This method can help reduce bias caused by underrepresentation or overrepresentation of certain groups in the data.
