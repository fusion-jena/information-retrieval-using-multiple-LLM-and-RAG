However, it is common in machine learning and deep learning to use several evaluation metrics to assess the model's performance. Some of these metrics include:

1. Accuracy: It is the ratio of the number of correct predictions to the total number of input samples. High accuracy indicates that the model performs well in general.

2. Precision: Precision is the ratio of true positive predictions (relevant samples that are correctly identified) to the total predicted positives (sum of true positives and false positives). High precision indicates that the model has a low false positive rate.

3. Recall (Sensitivity): Recall is the ratio of true positive predictions to the total actual positives (sum of true positives and false negatives). High recall indicates that the model has a low false negative rate.

4. F1-score: F1-score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a single metric that considers both aspects. It is a more balanced metric than accuracy, especially when dealing with imbalanced datasets.

5. Mean Absolute Error (MAE) or Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE): These metrics are particularly useful when evaluating regression problems. MAE measures the average absolute difference between predicted and actual values, while RMSE squares the differences before averaging, making it more sensitive to large errors.
