In general, when evaluating a deep learning model, several metrics can be used, such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score. Here's a brief description of each:

1. Accuracy: This is the ratio of the number of correct predictions to the total number of input samples. It works well when the classes are balanced; otherwise, it might not provide an accurate representation of the model's performance.

2. Precision: Also called Positive Predictive Value, precision is the ratio of true positives (correctly predicted positive samples) to the sum of true positives and false positives (incorrectly predicted positive samples). It is useful when the cost of a false positive is high.

3. Recall: Also known as Sensitivity or True Positive Rate, recall is the ratio of true positives to the sum of true positives and false negatives (incorrectly predicted negative samples). It is useful when the cost of a false negative is high.

4. F1 score: The F1 score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a single metric that considers both. It is a more balanced metric when dealing with imbalanced classes.

The kappa coefficient is a statistical measure of the agreement between two raters, which helps account for the possibility of the agreement occurring by chance. It is a more robust metric when dealing with imbalanced classes compared to overall accuracy.
