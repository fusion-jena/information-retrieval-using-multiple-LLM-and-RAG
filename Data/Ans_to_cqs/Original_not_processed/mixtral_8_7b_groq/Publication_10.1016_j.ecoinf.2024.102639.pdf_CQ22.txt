The performance of deep learning models, such as K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Support Vector Machine (SVM), and Principal Component Analysis-Support Vector Machine (PCA-SVM), can be evaluated using several metrics. These metrics include precision, recall, F-measure, and accuracy.

Precision measures the proportion of true positive predictions out of all positive predictions made by the model. It is calculated as the ratio of correctly predicted positive observations to the total predicted positives. High precision indicates that the model has a low false positive rate.

Recall, also known as sensitivity, measures the proportion of true positive predictions out of all actual positive observations. It is calculated as the ratio of correctly predicted positive observations to the total actual positives. High recall indicates that the model has a low false negative rate.

F-measure is the harmonic mean of precision and recall, which provides a balanced assessment of both measures. It is calculated as the ratio of twice the product of precision and recall to the sum of precision and recall.

Accuracy measures the proportion of correct predictions made by the model out of all predictions. It is calculated as the ratio of correctly predicted observations to the total observations. While accuracy is a commonly used metric, it may not always provide an accurate representation of the model's performance, especially when the dataset is imbalanced.

In the context of classifying the left-right movements of zebrafish and rats, the KNN model demonstrated high precision and recall in identifying left movements, while the SVM model outperformed in precision and recall for left movements. The PCA-SVM approach achieved remarkable accuracy rates of 98.07% for zebrafish and 98.15% for rats, highlighting its exceptional capability in interpreting complex behavioral data with minimal error.

Therefore, when evaluating the performance of deep learning models, it is essential to consider multiple metrics, including precision, recall, F-measure, and accuracy, to provide a comprehensive assessment of the model's performance.
