All  data  analysis  was  conducted  in  R  4.0.5  (R  Development  Core 
Team, 2021). Gaping data, expressed in proportion of aperture where 
0 is fully closed and 1 is fully opened, and recorded at 1 Hz (see Bertolini 
et al., 2021c), were averaged (mean) over 5 min in the Venice Lagoon 
and over 1 min in the Wadden Sea. This difference in averaging time was 
due to different lengths of deployment periods. Using 1 min averages 
would  make  the  Venice  files  too  large  to  easily  process,  while  in  the 
Wadden Sea this higher frequency was technical feasible. Daily curves of 
gaping for each sensor were then used as input in the analysis yielding 
1286 daily curves for the Venice Lagoon and 203 daily curves for the 
Wadden Sea.

The given context does not provide information about any specific deep learning model or its evaluation metrics. However, generally speaking, some common metrics used to evaluate the performance of deep learning models include accuracy, precision, recall, F1 score, ROC curve, and area under the curve (AUC). Accuracy measures the ratio of correct predictions made by the model out of all predictions. Precision measures the number of true positive predictions divided by the total predicted positives. Recall measures the number of true positive predictions divided by the total actual positives. The F1 score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall. The Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curve plots the True Positive Rate against the False Positive Rate, and the Area Under the Curve (AUC) provides a measure of how well the model can distinguish between classes. These metrics help assess the quality of the model's predictions and determine whether it is suitable for the intended application.
