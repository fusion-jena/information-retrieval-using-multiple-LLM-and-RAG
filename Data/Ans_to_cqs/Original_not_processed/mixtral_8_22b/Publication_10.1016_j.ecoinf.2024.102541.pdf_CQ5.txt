3.1.2. Neck network 

The PANet and BiFPN in the YOLOv5s architecture play pivotal roles 
in  feature  map  retention.  PANet  adopts  a  top-down  methodology, 
channelling  semantic  information  from  deeper,  high-level  layers  to 
shallower, low-level ones. Conversely, BiFPN employs a more iterative, 
bidirectional  fusion  strategy,  ensuring  both  deep  and  shallow  layers 
benefit from continuous bidirectional data flow. This approach provides 
a robust, versatile method for feature fusion, adaptable to various object 
recognition  challenges.  Following  the  principles  outlined,  (Tan  et  al., 
2020)  suggests  the  use  of  weighted  inputs,  enabling  the  network  to 
assess the significance of each feature. This concept is exemplified in Eq.
